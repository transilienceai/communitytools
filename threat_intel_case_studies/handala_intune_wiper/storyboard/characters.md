# Character Bible — Handala / Void Manticore Intune MDM Wiper

---

## OPERATOR KIAN — Handala Lead Attacker

**Physical Build**: Late 30s Iranian male. Lean, angular build — narrow shoulders, wiry frame that suggests endurance rather than strength. His body is the shape of a man who forgets to eat when working. Height: average, but appears smaller because of the perpetual hunch. Shaved head revealing the topography of his skull — the occipital ridge catches green light. Sharp jaw with a day of stubble, the kind that suggests "hasn't thought about it" rather than fashion. Olive skin with blue-black undertone that becomes pronounced under screen light.

**Face & Features**: Wears thin-framed rectangular glasses that catch and hold monitor reflections — sometimes you see the screen in his lenses before you see his eyes. Prominent cheekbones that create deep shadow pools under up-lighting. Nose: straight, narrow, with a slight leftward deviation from a break years ago. Left ear: green-tinted communications earpiece, always present, the cord disappearing into his jacket collar. His eyes are dark brown, almost black, and they don't dart — they track. Slow, deliberate eye movement. He reads a screen the way a predator watches prey.

**Hands**: Slender, long-fingered — a pianist's hands repurposed for keyboards. The tendons are visible on the back of the hand. Nails: short, clean, functional. His right index finger has a slight callus on the pad from years of mouse-clicking. When he types, his wrists float — he's a touch-typist, efficient, minimal wrist movement.

**Clothing**: Olive drab utility jacket over a dark crew-neck shirt, the shirt's collar just visible. The jacket is worn at the elbows, creased from habitual folding of arms — a garment of habit, not fashion. Two pockets on the chest, both buttoned. Pants: dark, nondescript. Shoes: never visible (he's always seated or shown from the waist up). Prayer beads on left wrist — amber-colored, 33 beads on a silk thread, warm-toned against cool green light. The beads are the only warm element on his person — they humanize him.

**Movement & Posture**: Hunched when working — shoulders curved inward, head tilted toward the screen at roughly 15 degrees, like a bird of prey studying the ground from a wire. His center of gravity shifts forward when engaged. When not typing, his left hand wraps around a tea glass (always tea, never coffee, always in a small tulip glass with a gold rim). His right rests on the mouse with index finger slightly curled — always ready to click. He never gestures broadly — economy of motion is his defining physical trait. When standing (Panel 05), the transformation is startling: he straightens fully, arms slightly spread, head back. The hunch was the working posture. The upright stance is the victory posture.

**Micro-expressions & Emotional Tells**: Kian doesn't smile. Ever. His "satisfaction" shows as a slight narrowing of the eyes — the outer corners compress by millimeters — and a barely perceptible tightening of the jaw. The face of a craftsman examining a joint that fits perfectly. Under stress (Panel 06, pre-trigger), a single bead of sweat forms at the right temple. No other physiological tells. His breathing is controlled — you never see his chest rise. His mouth is a neutral line, neither turned up nor down. He speaks rarely, and when he does his jaw barely moves.

**Light Interaction**: Screens are his primary and often only light source. The monitor up-light creates a ghoulish effect — shadows pooling in eye sockets, cheekbones thrown into sharp relief, the shaved head catching green-blue reflections like a dull mirror. In the abstract panels (04-05), green digital artifacts trail from his silhouette like corrupted data particles — visual representation of his digital contamination spreading.

**Narrative Function**: The patient predator. Never rushed, never emotional, never sloppy. He embodies "living off the land" — using the system's own tools, within the system's own rules. He is terrifying precisely because he is calm. His craft is indistinguishable from a legitimate admin's daily work.

## SARAH CHEN — Stryker CISO

**Physical Build**: Mid-40s Chinese-American woman. Medium build, athletic posture that conveys habitual discipline — she was a runner in college and still carries that upright carriage. Not imposing physically, but her presence fills rooms through posture and stillness. She occupies space with intention, not mass.

**Face & Features**: Hair: jet black, thick, with a single streak of silver at the left temple that appeared the year she took the CISO role. Pulled into a severe bun when working — not a strand out of place. In Panel 08, loose on the pillow: the only intimate moment, the bun released, hair fanning across dark fabric. Rectangular glasses with thin titanium frames — wire-thin, almost invisible from a distance but they define her face in close-up. Without them (Panel 11), her face looks naked, exposed, younger by a decade. Clean jawline with slight definition at the masseter — she clenches her jaw under stress. Slight crow's feet at the eyes — laugh lines repurposed by years of staring at dashboards.

**Hands**: Her hands are her emotional barometer — more expressive than her face. Flat-palmed on a desk surface means analytical thinking. Finger-pointing means directing, commanding. Fists clenched at sides means processing overwhelming input. Open palms pressed to face means system overload — overwhelmed. When she holds a dry-erase marker (Panel 12), her grip is firm, controlled — the grip of someone who has regained agency.

**Clothing**: Dark charcoal blazer over a white collared shirt — the blazer is her armor. Always buttoned when she enters a room. It comes off when the crisis exceeds what armor can protect against. Stryker ID badge on a lanyard, slightly worn at the edges. The badge swings when she walks quickly — a pendulum measuring her urgency. Sleeves get rolled progressively as crisis deepens: first the shirt cuffs appear, then full forearm. Simple analog watch on left wrist — she doesn't trust devices she can't wind.

**Movement & Posture**: In command: spine straight, chin level, feet planted shoulder-width, weight forward on the balls of her feet. She looks like she's about to step toward the problem. Processing bad news: rigid stillness, fists clenched at sides, one foot forward — the body caught between "fight" and "freeze." Defeated (Panel 11): elbows on knees, forehead pressed against palms, badge dangling toward the floor, the spine curved — the first time we see her slouch. She makes the institutional bench look like the loneliest place on earth.

**Micro-expressions & Emotional Tells**: Her competence shows in her eyes — rapid lateral scanning when reading dashboards, a slight lift of the left eyebrow when she spots a pattern others haven't. Her shock (Panel 09) shows as a fractional widening of the eyes and a jaw muscle tightening visible at the temple. She doesn't cry. She doesn't scream. She goes still — absolute stillness, the kind that makes everyone else in the room stop moving too. Her "I understand now" expression (Panel 12) is the inverse of her shock: eyes slightly narrowed, chin dipped, the focused certainty of someone who has mapped the enemy.

**Light Interaction**: Blue phone light in the bedroom (Panel 08) catches the silver streak like a beacon. Red SOC light (Panel 09) makes her look bloodless, the red washing out her skin tone in monitor reflections. Emergency fluorescent (Panel 11) drains all color — she becomes grayscale, matching the institutional emptiness. Natural conference room light (Panel 12) is the first time we see her in normal illumination — the color returns to her face with the clarity.

**Arc**: Competence → shock → helplessness → determination. She IS the story's emotional spine. Her journey from sleep to war room to empty hallway to whiteboard maps the complete human response to catastrophic infrastructure betrayal.

## COLONEL HASSAN — MOIS Handler

**Physical Build**: Late 50s Iranian male. Stocky, broad-shouldered, barrel-chested — the build of a man who was once military-fit and has since settled into the mass of administrative power. He carries weight in his torso and neck. His body communicates permanence — he does not move quickly or need to.

**Face & Features**: Full face with heavy jowls that bracket a firm mouth. Silver-streaked mustache, trimmed military-precise — the only personal grooming indulgence in an otherwise austere presentation. Deep-set eyes under heavy brows — the kind of brow that creates permanent shadow over the eyes, making his gaze hard to read from a distance. His forehead is lined: three deep horizontal furrows that compress further when he's delivering orders. Government pin on his left lapel — small, circular, gold-toned, catching the desk lamp.

**Clothing**: Dark suit, white shirt, no tie — the uniform of Iranian government authority. Good quality fabric but nothing flashy — state-issued respectability. Jacket always buttoned. Shirt collar crisp. Everything pressed. He dresses like a man who regards clothing as uniform, not expression.

**Movement & Posture**: Minimal. Hands flat on the mahogany desk, fingers spread wide — the gesture of a decision transmitted, not debated. He doesn't lean forward (that would signal eagerness or uncertainty). He sits upright, spine against the chair back, chin tilted slightly down — looking at the person below his eyeline. Everything about his posture says "I am above." When he lifts one hand from the desk, it's to make a single decisive gesture — a flat palm rotated, meaning "proceed."

**Micro-expressions**: One corner of his mouth slightly downturned — not anger, not sadness. Resignation. The permanent expression of a man who has authorized operations before, knows what they cost in human terms, and has processed the moral weight into a filing cabinet in his mind labeled "necessary." His eyes don't harden — they're already hard. They simply... confirm.

**Light Interaction**: Warm desk lamp from lower-left catches the planes of his face and the silver threads in his mustache, giving him an almost painterly golden quality. Cool blue-white from the news footage on the TV behind creates a rim light on his shoulders and the crown of his head. The competing warm/cool light sources create a visual metaphor: civilized governance from the front, cold operational reality from behind.

**Appears in**: Panel 01 (primary), possibly referenced in Panel 12's whiteboard. Two panels maximum. He gives the order and disappears from the narrative. The hand that sets the machine in motion and never touches it again.

## THE INTUNE CONSOLE — Anthropomorphized Visual Motif

**Visual Identity**: Not a character but a visual entity that undergoes a transformation arc across the story. Begins as the Microsoft Intune management interface — Azure blue (#0078D4), clean, corporate, organized, trustworthy. Grid layouts with device inventory tables. Compliance dashboards showing green checkmarks. The visual language of IT safety, order, and control. It looks like something designed to protect you.

**Transformation Sequence**:
- Panel 00 (center): The Intune logo cracked, green binary bleeding through — foreshadowing.
- Panel 04: First green veins appear in the blue interface. The corruption is organic, biological — like mold threading through bread, like plant roots cracking concrete. It spreads from where Kian walks.
- Panel 05: The console rendered at architectural scale — a brutalist cathedral of device management. 30% corrupted green, 70% still Azure blue. The green climbs upward like a rising tide. The wipe command form glows red at the base.
- Post-wipe: The console appears as a dark, inert screen. Spent. The weapon discarded.

**Visual Metaphor**: Infrastructure trust made visible and then destroyed. The corruption is not a "hack" — the doors opened with correct keys. The commands were legitimate admin actions. The horror: the system functioned exactly as designed, in the wrong hands.

**Color Journey**: Azure blue (#0078D4) → green veins emerging → green spreading (30/70 split) → green dominant → fully weaponized green → dark/inert post-wipe.

**Scale**: In Panel 05, rendered at skyscraper scale — Kian a tiny silhouette at its base. One human. 200,000 devices. The management plane dwarfs its new master.

## THE HANDS — Device Victim Motif

**Concept**: Diverse human hands holding devices in various states of death. Never attached to full figures — they exist as close-up vignettes within Panel 07's cascade sub-panels. They represent the human scale of 200,000 wipes without requiring 200,000 characters. The absence of faces forces the viewer to project their own identity into each scene.

**Hand 1 — The Surgeon**: Blue nitrile surgical gloves, sterile, with the powder-free matte finish. Reaching toward a tablet on an articulating arm — fingers spread in the universal "what just happened?" gesture. Glove creases visible at the knuckle joints from the stretching motion. A sterile field bracelet visible at the wrist. The hand is mid-reach, caught between the surgical field and the failing device — torn between the patient and the screen. Implications: a life may depend on what this screen shows next, and it's showing nothing.

**Hand 2 — The Factory Worker**: Bare hand, broad-palmed, callused along the base of the fingers from repetitive gripping. A hi-vis vest cuff visible at the wrist — neon yellow with reflective stripe. Pulling a personal phone from a trouser pocket. The phone screen shows a wipe progress screen. The hand grips the phone tightly — the involuntary squeeze of alarm. This person's personal device was enrolled in BYOD. The attack followed them home.

**Hand 3 — The Paramedic**: Reaching for a radio mic clipped to the sun visor mount. The Lifenet EKG transmission screen in the background — mounted on the ambulance dash — is blank. A chronograph watch on the wrist with elapsed-time bezel. The hand is steady, trained to act under pressure. But you can see the tendons taut under the skin — controlled tension. The patient's cardiac data was being relayed to the hospital. It's not being relayed anymore. Radio is the fallback.

**Hand 4 — The Parent**: Delicate hand holding a personal iPhone, the screen showing a device erasure countdown. A simple gold wedding band on the ring finger. Behind the phone, slightly out of focus: a child's crayon drawing taped to the kitchen counter — stick figures, a house, a sun. This hand represents BYOD taken to its worst conclusion. Personal photos. Personal contacts. The 2FA codes needed to recover everything. All being erased. The attack reached through the corporate perimeter and into someone's kitchen.

**Function**: Four hands, four micro-stories, four scales of impact. OR → factory → ambulance → kitchen. The geometric reach of 200,000 wipes, compressed into four gestures of helplessness.

## THE SOC ENSEMBLE — Background Team

**Composition**: 3-4 recurring analysts who populate the SOC. They appear as a collective in Panels 09-10 and one distinctive member carries through to Panel 12. They are the chorus — their reactions calibrate the viewer's emotional register.

**The Red-Haired Analyst (primary recurring)**: Male, early 30s, Irish-American heritage visible in the pale, freckle-dusted complexion. Distinctive copper-red hair, slightly unkempt — the hair of someone who hasn't been home in 12 hours. The red color makes him instantly locatable in crowd scenes. Pale skin that flushes pink-red under stress, visible even under red emergency lighting. Always has energy drink cans nearby — silver and green cans arranged in compulsive patterns on his desk (triangle of 3 in Panel 09). Wears a dark hoodie under a badge lanyard — the hoodie-under-badge combo marks him as the youngest generation in the SOC. His screens consistently show network topology maps with device nodes — and in the crisis panels, those nodes are winking dark one by one. His emotional register: wide-eyed focus, mouth slightly open, the expression of a person who processes shock by observing harder. In Panel 12: he's the one taking a photo of the whiteboard with his phone — the documentarian, the one who will carry this forward.

**Analyst 2 — The Veteran**: Older male, early 50s. Gray at the temples, reading glasses pushed up on his forehead where they live when he's not actively reading fine print. Weathered face that's seen previous incidents but nothing at this scale. In Panel 10, his hand covers his mouth — the unconscious gesture of a professional who recognizes the magnitude before the words catch up. He's seated with both feet flat on the floor — grounded, stable, the anchor in the room even when he's horrified.

**Analyst 3 — The Communicator**: Female, late 20s. Dark hair pulled into a practical ponytail. On a phone in Panel 09 — she's the conduit to the outside world, calling upstream to leadership, calling partners, calling legal. Her body language is forward-leaning, one hand pressing the phone to her ear, the other hand flat on the desk with fingers splayed — multitasking under adrenaline.

**Collective Function**: They form the human ecosystem of a SOC under siege. Their horror validates the viewer's horror. When experienced professionals react with open mouths and covered faces, the viewer's calibration shifts: this isn't a drill, this isn't recoverable quickly, this is the worst day of these people's careers. They ground the abstract catastrophe in recognizable professional distress.
