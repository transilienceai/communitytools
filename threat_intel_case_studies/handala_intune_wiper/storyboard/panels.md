## Panel 00: HEADER — "The Management Plane"

**Aspect**: 16:9 wide (carousel header)

**Composition**: Triptych layout divided into three vertical thirds.

**Left Third**: KIAN seen from behind, seated before a wall of monitors in a dark room. His silhouette is small, hunched, predatory — the green glow of his screens reflects off the back of his shaved head and the olive fabric of his jacket. Digital rain of green characters cascades down the monitors. His posture is curved inward, shoulders tight — a raptor on a perch. The prayer beads on his left wrist catch a single green highlight.

**Center Third**: The Microsoft Intune logo, rendered large and monolithic, cracked down the middle like shattered safety glass. Through the crack, green binary code bleeds outward like an arterial wound — the data hemorrhaging from a broken trust boundary. The crack emanates from a cursor arrow that pierces the logo — the click that broke everything. Shards of the logo float outward, each reflecting a tiny fragment of a device screen.

**Right Third**: SARAH CHEN stands before a wall of monitors entirely consumed by red — cascading alert windows stacked so deep the SIEM dashboard beneath is barely visible. Her blazer is off, sleeves rolled to the elbow, one hand pressed flat against the glass of a monitor as if trying to reach through it. Her reflection is ghostly in the red-lit glass — a transparent Sarah overlaid on the alert cascade. Her silver temple streak catches the red light.

**Lighting**: Three distinct color zones established immediately. Left: green glow (attacker). Center: cold white fracture light (broken trust). Right: red wash (defender's crisis). The three colors of the entire story previewed in one image.

**Mood**: Duality. The attacker's methodical calm vs. the defender's escalating crisis. The broken tool between them — the cracked Intune logo as the fulcrum of the story.

**Typography Space**: Large clear area across the top third for title text overlay. Bottom strip below the triptych for subtitle/date.

## Panel 01: "The Order" — Tehran, February 28, 2026

**Aspect**: 3:4 portrait

**Camera**: Extreme low angle (worm's-eye), looking up at COLONEL HASSAN from below desk level. Hassan appears enormous, authoritative, immovable — the geometry of power rendered in perspective.

**Scene**: A ministerial office in Tehran. Wood-paneled walls, the edge of a Persian rug visible in the foreground blur. This is civilized — not a bunker, not a war room. Behind Hassan, a flat-screen mounted on the wall shows grainy news footage: smoke rising from a building, the distinctive red-orange of flames against night sky. The Minab school strike — 175 dead. The footage casts a warm orange glow that competes with the cold fluorescent desk lamp.

**Character Detail**: Hassan's hands are flat on the dark mahogany desk surface, fingers spread wide — the gesture of a decision made, not a decision being weighed. His government pin on the left lapel catches the desk lamp. His silver-streaked mustache is trimmed military-precise. His eyes do not look at the screen behind him — he has already seen it, already processed it. He looks directly at someone below frame, giving the order. One corner of his mouth is slightly downturned. Not anger. Resignation. The face of a man who has made this kind of decision before and will make it again.

**Foreground**: The blurred edge of a phone receiver lifted from its cradle by an unseen hand at the bottom-left corner. The cord coils downward. The call is being made. The dominoes begin.

**Lighting**: Warm desk lamp from lower-left catches the planes of Hassan's face, emphasizing the heavy brow and jaw. Cool blue-white from the TV behind creates a rim light on his shoulders and the top of his head. Deep shadows pool in the room corners and under the desk — the darkness of institutional secrecy.

**Mood**: Cold authority. The machinery of state violence setting into motion. The viewer should feel the weight of hierarchy pressing down from above — this is not a hacker's basement; this is an office where nations make decisions that ripple across 79 countries.

## Panel 02: "The Craft" — Undisclosed Location, March 1-8, 2026

**Aspect**: 3:4 portrait

**Camera**: Over-the-shoulder shot from behind and slightly above KIAN. We see past the curve of his shaved head and the collar of his olive jacket to the screen in front of him. The screen fills the middle third of the panel and is the brightest element.

**Scene**: A small, cluttered operations room. Not glamorous — exposed cable ducts on a low ceiling, mismatched monitors on a long desk, empty tea glasses with sugar residue on their rims. Three or four other figures are visible in the deep background, faces obscured by shadow and the glare of their own screens, hunched over stations. This feels real — cramped, utilitarian, the working conditions of state-sponsored operators who are workers, not Bond villains.

**The Screen**: Kian is composing a phishing email. The screen shows a pixel-perfect replica of a Microsoft 365 admin portal login page — indistinguishable from legitimate. Below it in a second window, a text editor with the email template: Microsoft logo, formatted paragraphs, a blue "Review Activity" button. In a third tab barely visible at the edge, a spreadsheet of harvested email addresses. The craftsmanship is meticulous.

**Character Detail**: Kian's right hand rests on the mouse, index finger slightly curled — about to click but not yet. His left hand wraps around a tea glass, the amber liquid catching the green screen light. Prayer beads dangle from his left wrist. His posture is relaxed, unhurried — this is day 4 of a week-long campaign. In the reflection of his thin rectangular glasses, the faintest mirror image of the screen is visible. The green earpiece in his left ear glows faintly.

**Foreground Depth**: A pack of cigarettes and crumpled cellophane sit on the desk edge nearest the viewer, slightly out of focus. An ashtray with two stubs. A second tea glass, empty, sugar residue. The detritus of patient work.

**Lighting**: Screens are the only light source. Kian's face is lit from front-below by his monitor — the classic ghoulish up-light effect that makes eye sockets darken and cheekbones sharpen. The room behind fades to near-black within two meters. Green is the dominant color cast — everything bathes in terminal green.

**Mood**: Meticulous patience. Craft. This is a tradesman at work — the same focused calm as a watchmaker assembling a mechanism that will, when wound, destroy everything it touches.

## Panel 03: "The Bite" — Stryker HQ, Kalamazoo, MI, March 11, 3:12 AM EDT

**Aspect**: 3:4 portrait

**Camera**: Extreme close-up. The entire panel is a laptop screen and the hand operating the trackpad below it. No face visible — the victim is deliberately anonymous. Any IT admin. Anywhere. The facelessness makes the viewer project themselves into the scene.

**Scene**: A laptop screen showing a Microsoft 365 email, open and filling the display. The email looks legitimate — Microsoft branding, professional formatting, a notification about unusual sign-in activity on an admin account. Urgency in the message body. A blue button for action (text area left blank for overlay). The email is a masterwork of social engineering: just enough urgency to override caution at 3 AM.

**The Critical Detail**: At the very edge of the screen, barely visible in the URL bar, the domain is one character off from legitimate. But the admin's eyes aren't on the URL bar. They're on the urgent warning. This detail rewards close readers — the "spot the difference" that separates safety from catastrophe.

**Character Detail**: The hand on the trackpad. Gender-neutral, could be anyone. A wedding ring on the ring finger — this person has a family, a life, a tomorrow that's about to get much harder. A coffee cup ring stain on the desk surface beside the laptop. It's 3 AM — woken by an alert, operating on instinct not analysis. The fingertip is pressing down on the trackpad — the moment of the click, captured in freeze-frame.

**Visual Metaphor**: From the point of contact where the finger presses the trackpad, hairline green cracks spider outward through the laptop's physical surface — like ice fracturing under weight, like a windshield hit by a stone. Subtle, almost missable on first viewing. The green glow of the cracks is faint but unmistakable. This is the moment the entire attack chain activates.

**Lighting**: The laptop screen is the only light source in a dark room. Blue-white illumination catches the underside of the hand, the trackpad surface, the desk edge. The green cracks glow with their own faint luminescence — the only color that isn't blue-white.

**Mood**: The mundane catastrophe. The most devastating cyberattack of 2026 begins with a sleepy IT admin clicking an email at 3 AM. No drama. No music sting. Just a click. The ordinariness IS the horror.

## Panel 04: "The Keys to the Kingdom" — Digital/Abstract

**Aspect**: 3:4 portrait, FULL BLEED (no panel borders — art extends to edges)

**Camera**: Straight-on, centered, symmetrical composition deliberately echoing religious iconography — a figure passing through a sacred portal.

**Scene**: An abstract digital space. The Microsoft Entra ID portal reimagined as a massive corporate vault door — Azure blue (#0078D4), smooth, monolithic, bearing the Entra ID shield logo. The door is open. Not blown, not hacked — opened with the right key. It swings inward on its hinges exactly as designed. There is no evidence of force. This is the horror.

**Character Detail**: KIAN walks through the doorway, seen from behind. He is small against the massive door — the scale emphasizes what he has just gained access to. His silhouette trails green digital artifacts like corrupted data bleeding off him into the clean blue space. In one hand, held casually at his side, a keycard/credential that glows (text area blank for "Global Administrator" overlay). He walks calmly. Not running. He belongs here now.

**Visual Metaphor**: The clean Azure blue of the Entra ID space is beginning to transform. Where Kian has walked, the floor shows green veining — like mold spreading through bread, like corruption propagating through healthy tissue. The transformation is organic, biological, disease-like. Behind him: blue is still pure. Ahead of him: green tendrils reach forward anticipatorily. The infection follows the intruder.

**Lighting**: The Entra portal glows from within — cold Azure blue light. Kian is silhouetted against this glow, creating a dramatic rim-light on his figure's edges. Green emanates from the corruption spreading at his feet and trailing behind him. Two-color split: blue above, green below and growing.

**Mood**: Violation. Desecration of a trusted space. The viewer should feel the wrongness — a predator in the sanctuary. The vault door opened with the right key feels worse than if it had been blown open. Legitimate access used for illegitimate purpose.

## Panel 05: "The Management Plane" — The Intune Console

**Aspect**: 3:4 portrait, FULL BLEED

**Camera**: Extreme low angle, almost directly upward. KIAN stands at the base of the panel, his back to us, head tilted up. Above him — filling 80% of the vertical space — the INTUNE CONSOLE rises like a monolith.

**Scene**: The most abstract panel. The Intune console is rendered not as a laptop screen but as an architectural space — a cathedral of device management. Rows and columns of device entries stretch upward into darkness like a database turned into a brutalist skyscraper. Each row represents a device — there are hundreds of thousands of them. The sheer visual density of the grid communicates scale without numbers. Device management dashboards, fleet inventories, compliance policy panels form the facade of this digital edifice.

**The Corruption**: Green veins visibly thread through the blue interface. The Azure blue is being colonized. At Kian's level (bottom of panel), interface elements are already green — fully corrupted. Higher up, still blue — but the green is climbing, branching, spreading. The transformation is approximately 30% complete, creating a visible gradient: green at the base, transitional in the middle, blue at the heights. The infection propagates upward.

**Character Detail**: Kian's posture has transformed. No longer hunched over a desk — he stands straight, arms slightly spread, head tilted back. A figure at the base of a monument. His "I am become Death" moment — the operator surveying the scope of what he now commands. The prayer beads on his wrist are the only warm-colored element in the entire panel — amber against the green-and-blue chromatic battle.

**Foreground**: At Kian's feet, the "floor" of this digital space shows the first wipe command form being composed — fields for device scope, action type, confirmation status. It glows angry red (#FF0033) at the bottom of the panel — the first appearance of the crisis color. The weapon is being loaded.

**Lighting**: Blue from above (unconquered console), green from the sides (corrupted zones), faint red from below (the wipe command). Three-color lighting creates otherworldly chromatic tension. Kian is a dark silhouette caught between these competing lights.

**Mood**: Terrible power. Vertigo at scale. One human standing before 200,000 devices. The management plane is vast, and it now belongs to the attacker.

## Panel 06: "3:30 AM" — The Trigger

**Aspect**: 3:4 portrait

**Camera**: FOUR VERTICAL SUB-PANELS, each a progressively tighter close-up. The "decompressed" manga technique — stretching a single second across four frames to make the reader feel every microsecond of the point of no return.

**Sub-panel 1** (top, widest): Kian's face in profile, left-facing. His eyes are steady, reflecting green light from the screen. His jaw is set. One bead of sweat at the right temple — the only sign of physiological arousal in the entire story. The clock on his screen reads 3:30 AM. His right index finger is poised above the Enter key, hovering. The moment before.

**Sub-panel 2**: Just his right hand and the keyboard section beneath it. The index finger is descending. The Enter key is slightly depressed under the weight. Green backlight bleeds upward between the key gaps like magma between tectonic plates. Every other key is dark — only this one matters. The prayer beads are visible at the wrist edge.

**Sub-panel 3**: Extreme close-up — the fingertip making contact with the key surface. The key is fully depressed, bottomed out. From the point of contact, speed lines radiate outward in a starburst pattern — the manga "impact frame," the moment of irreversible force. Green light flares from the key.

**Sub-panel 4** (bottom, full width): PURE BLACK. Nothing. No image. Absolute darkness. This is the "ma" — the negative space between the keystroke and the consequence. A held breath. The reader pauses here because there is nothing to look at, and in that pause, dread builds. The silence before the scream.

**Lighting**: Green glow in sub-panels 1-3, progressively more intense — dim reflection on face, brighter on hand, flare at impact. Sub-panel 4: void. No light. Nothing.

**Mood**: The point of no return, rendered in slow motion. The reader knows what comes next. The black void of sub-panel 4 is the last moment before the world changes. Dread lives in the emptiness.

## Panel 07: "Cascade" — Global Wipe, March 11, 2026

**Aspect**: 3:4 portrait

**Camera**: SIX DIAGONAL SUB-PANELS arranged like falling dominoes — each tilted at increasing Dutch angles (5° → 10° → 15° → 20° → 25° → 30°). The escalating visual instability mirrors systemic collapse. Each sub-panel is a different location, a different victim.

**Sub-panel 1** (5° tilt): A hospital operating room. Bright surgical lights overhead. A SURGEON in scrubs looks down at a tablet mounted on an articulating arm — the screen shows a Stryker surgical planning interface. Mid-procedure. The screen flickers — a loading spinner appears. The surgeon's gloved hand reaches toward it, fingers spread.

**Sub-panel 2** (10° tilt): Same OR, tighter frame. The tablet screen is now blue — Windows reset screen. The surgeon's eyes above the surgical mask are wide. A second staff member in background looks at their own device. The draped patient on the table is visible — anonymous, mid-operation. The stakes are visceral.

**Sub-panel 3** (15° tilt): Cork, Ireland. A factory floor. Workers in hi-vis vests at a manufacturing line. Laptops integrated into the line are going dark, one by one, left to right — a visible wave of failure sweeping through the frame like dominoes.

**Sub-panel 4** (20° tilt): An office cubicle farm. Every monitor simultaneously displays the Handala logo — stylized hand silhouette, green on black. An employee stands frozen at their desk, arms at their sides, staring. Others are turning to each other.

**Sub-panel 5** (25° tilt): Ambulance interior. A PARAMEDIC reaches for the radio mic. The Lifenet EKG transmission screen — mounted in the vehicle, showing cardiac telemetry being relayed to hospital — has gone blank mid-transmission. The patient on the stretcher is blurred in background.

**Sub-panel 6** (30° tilt): A PARENT'S HAND holding a personal iPhone. The screen shows an "iPhone will be erased" countdown. A child's crayon drawing is visible on the kitchen counter behind the phone. This is not a corporate device. This is someone's life. BYOD made it a target.

**Lighting**: Progressive darkening. Sub-panel 1: bright surgical whites. Sub-panel 3: industrial fluorescents. Sub-panel 6: dim kitchen, phone screen the only light illuminating the parent's hand.

**Mood**: Geometric escalation as visual cascade. Operating room to factory to office to ambulance to private kitchen — the attack's reach extends beyond corporate walls into personal lives. The increasing Dutch tilt creates visual nausea, disorientation. Scale spiraling beyond control.

**Aspect**: 3:4 portrait

**Camera**: Close-up, shallow depth of field. SARAH CHEN's face fills the frame, lying on a pillow. Her phone is ringing — visible in the extreme foreground, blurred, its screen casting a cold blue rectangle of light across the pillow and the left side of her face.

**Scene**: The split-second between sleep and crisis. Sarah's bedroom is barely visible — dark shapes of furniture, the edge of a nightstand, a water glass catching a pinpoint of phone light. The phone vibrates against the wooden nightstand surface (small vibration lines radiating from the device). Caller ID area is blank for text overlay.

**Character Detail**: Sarah's hair is down for the only time in the entire comic — loose on the pillow, the severe bun released. It humanizes her completely. The silver streak at her left temple is visible against the dark pillowcase. Her expression is the micro-moment before comprehension: right eye still closed, left eye cracking open to catch the phone light, brow beginning to furrow, lips slightly parted. She doesn't know yet. **Visual Detail**: On the nightstand beside the phone: her rectangular titanium glasses (folded), the Stryker ID badge (lanyard coiled like a sleeping snake), a charging cable plugged into a second phone (work phone, already showing a red notification badge — the number high enough to be alarming). The details tell you who this person is without a word of exposition.

**Lighting**: Near-total darkness. The phone screen is the only light source — blue-white, casting hard shadows across Sarah's face. Half-lit, half in shadow. The blue light on her skin should feel cold, invasive, clinical — the light of bad news arriving.

**Mood**: The most intimate, human panel in the comic. No technology spectacle, no action. Just a person being woken into a nightmare she doesn't yet understand. The quiet before the storm. The viewer should feel protective of her.

Every monitor is red — cascading alert windows stacked so deep that earlier alerts are buried. The SIEM dashboard is unreadable beneath them.

**Scene**: The SOC in controlled pandemonium. Overhead fluorescents supplemented by red rotating emergency beacons. Six to eight analysts at stations — some standing, some on phones, one with head in hands. The RED-HAIRED ANALYST is closest to camera, seated, three empty energy drink cans in a triangle on his desk. His screens show a network map with nodes winking dark in real time — each one a device wipe propagating.

**Character Detail**: Sarah's blazer is back on — she came dressed for war. But it's slightly askew, buttons misaligned from being thrown on in the dark. Her Stryker badge swings from the lanyard. Her posture is rigid — both fists clenched at her sides, one foot forward. She is about to take charge, but for this frozen moment she is absorbing the scope. Her reflection is visible in the nearest monitor glass — face lit red, expression grim.

**Background Detail**: On one monitor, visible on close inspection: the Intune admin console showing wipe commands propagating — the tool that should be their ally is the weapon. Another monitor shows a world map with red dots blooming across 79 countries. The map is nearly covered.

**Foreground Depth**: A knocked-over coffee mug on the nearest desk, brown liquid pooling toward a keyboard. A phone receiver off its hook, coiled cord dangling. The detritus of sudden crisis — the before-world interrupted.

**Lighting**: RED. Dominant, overwhelming red from the monitors and emergency beacons. Blue-white fluorescent overheads create harsh shadow contrasts on faces and surfaces. No green in this panel — the attacker's color is entirely absent. This is purely the defender's nightmare rendered in crisis-red.

**Mood**: Information overload. The helplessness of watching a system you are supposed to protect destroy itself using its own administrative tools. Sarah's rigid posture is the internal battle between panic and training. The wall of red should feel like a physical force pressing down.

## Panel 10: "Two Hundred Thousand" — The Number

**Aspect**: 3:4 portrait

**Camera**: Extreme close-up of a single monitor screen, straight-on. The monitor bezel is visible at the edges. The glass is reflective — and in the reflection, ghostly transparent faces of the SOC team stare at this screen.

**Scene**: The monitor displays a dashboard counter. The number dominates the center in a large digital font (text area blank for overlay — visually suggesting a six-digit number 200,000+). Below it, a label area (blank for "Devices Wiped" overlay). A progress indicator shows the count still climbing. A graph beneath tracks the wipe rate over time — a near-vertical line that went from zero to catastrophe in under 30 minutes.

**The Reflected Faces**: The emotional core. Three to four faces visible in the monitor glass, semi-transparent, overlapping with the data behind them. SARAH center-reflected — rectangular glasses catching the red counter light. The RED-HAIRED ANALYST to the left, mouth open. An OLDER ANALYST to the right, hand covering his mouth. The reflections are ghostly, spectral — humans merged with data, trapped behind glass. They are both looking at the number and becoming part of it.

**Visual Detail**: The monitor bezel has a small Stryker asset tag sticker. Below the main counter, smaller counters show breakdowns (blank for overlay) — by device type, by region, by time elapsed. Each dimension of catastrophe quantified. The graph's vertical line is almost a wall — zero to maximum in no time at all.

**Lighting**: The red glow of the counter number is the dominant light, casting everything in crimson. The reflected faces are lit by this same red — bloodless, spectral, washed of humanity. The only light in the panel comes from the screen displaying the worst number these people have ever seen.

**Mood**: Scale made real. Not through action or chaos, but through a single number and the faces of professionals trying to comprehend it. The "that can't be right" moment. The viewer should feel the same numbness — the cognitive dissonance of a number too large to feel.

## Panel 11: "56,000 Idle" — The Silence After

**Aspect**: 3:4 portrait

**Camera**: Deep one-point perspective down a long corporate corridor. Vanishing point dead center. The hallway stretches into darkness. This is the "ma" panel — the negative space, the silence after.

**Scene**: A Stryker office hallway, morning. Overhead fluorescent lights on emergency power — every other fixture dark, creating a rhythmic light-and-shadow stripe pattern down the corridor. The hallway is empty. Doors closed. No one is here. Emergency exit signs glow red at the far end. A fire extinguisher cabinet provides a spot of red on the wall. The floor is polished enough to reflect the light-shadow stripes — the corridor doubled beneath itself.

**The Single Figure**: SARAH CHEN sits on a bench against the wall, roughly one-third from the bottom of the panel. She is small in the vast corridor — dwarfed by the architectural emptiness. Her blazer is draped over the bench beside her. Elbows on knees, forehead pressed against her palms. The Stryker badge dangles from her neck toward the floor. The glasses are off, held loosely in one hand — for the first time we see her without them. She looks smaller, younger, more human. More vulnerable.

**Visual Detail**: Beside her on the bench: her tablet, screen dark. Not broken — wiped. Useless. A metaphor for her role. On the floor near the bench, a single sheet of paper — someone printed something before the printers went down. It's face-down. We don't know what it says. The bench is institutional — metal legs, wooden slats. It was not designed for sitting on. It was designed for waiting.

**Lighting**: Emergency fluorescents — alternating light and dark bands down the corridor. Cool, institutional, heartless. No green, no red — the color-coding is over. Just gray-white and deep shadow. At the far end, through a window: the first rays of dawn. Golden (#FFD700) but distant. Unreachable. A future that feels far away.

**Mood**: Devastation. The quiet kind. The morning after. 56,000 workers sent home. The building is a shell. The CISO sits on a bench in a half-dark hallway because there is nothing left to defend. This panel should hurt. The emptiness of the corridor IS the story. The "ma" carries the weight.

## Panel 12: "The Kill Chain" — Whiteboard, 48 Hours Later

**Aspect**: 16:9 wide (carousel final image)

**Camera**: Medium shot from slight angle (not straight-on, approximately 15° off-axis). The whiteboard fills the upper 70% of the panel. SARAH stands to the right, marker in hand, having just finished drawing.

**Scene**: A conference room. SARAH has mapped the entire attack on a whiteboard — the kill chain rendered in dry-erase marker style, hand-drawn (visually contrasting with the manga rendering of everything else). This is the meta-moment: the story explains itself.

**The Whiteboard**: Hand-drawn flowchart showing the attack progression:
- Geopolitical trigger → MOIS orders → Target selection
- Phishing campaign → Credential harvest → Entra ID access
- Intune console → Remote wipe all devices → Impact cascade
- Impact branches: corporate devices, BYOD phones, Lifenet EKG, recovery path destroyed

Annotations in different marker colors around the diagram:
- Red marker: "NO MALWARE USED" (circled, underlined)
- Green marker: "Living Off the Land — legitimate admin tools"
- Blue marker: MITRE technique IDs
- Black marker: "Attack duration: <30 minutes. Preparation: 10 days."

Post-it notes stuck to the whiteboard: "Multi-admin approval?", "BYOD policy review", "Intune hardening sprint", "Tabletop exercise: MDM compromise"

**Character Detail**: Sarah's marker hand is still raised near the board — she has just drawn the last arrow. Fresh blazer, clean. The glasses are back on. Her jaw is set. Spine straight. She can't undo the attack, but she can map it, comprehend it, make sure the next defender sees it coming. Helplessness has converted to understanding. This is her resolution.

**Background**: Three to four people seated at a conference table in the lower portion of the panel, taking notes. One on a new laptop (unmanaged, brought from home). One holds a personal phone (also unmanaged). The org is running on borrowed equipment. The RED-HAIRED ANALYST is there, taking a photo of the whiteboard with his phone — preserving the knowledge.

**Lighting**: Bright conference room fluorescents. Normal, clean, institutional white light. The first fully-lit panel in the entire comic. After 11 panels of darkness and chromatic extremity, the normalcy of the light is jarring — and somehow comforting. The crisis is over. The reckoning begins.

**Mood**: Clarity. Understanding. Resolve. The attack mapped in its entirety — every step visible, every failure point identified. Knowledge as the first step toward prevention. The story ends not in despair but in determination. The whiteboard is the weapon against the next attack.
