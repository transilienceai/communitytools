# Physical — Tailgating / Piggybacking

## When this applies

Authorized physical security assessment to test whether employees challenge unfamiliar people entering controlled areas. Common at office buildings, data centers, secured floors, and badge-controlled entrances.

## Technique

Enter a controlled area by following an authorized person through the door, either covertly (tailgating) or with their explicit help (piggybacking). Pretexts smooth the interaction.

## Steps

1. Recon entrances, peak-traffic windows, smoke-break exits, delivery doors.
2. Observe badge designs, lanyard styles, and how badges are presented at readers.
3. Choose pretext: hands-full delivery, contractor, intern on first day, smoker returning.
4. Approach during peak flow; rely on social pressure to keep door open.
5. Once inside, document boundary crossings with timestamps and zones reached.
6. Exit cleanly; capture the path and any credentials/devices observed.

### Pretexts

```
- Carrying packages / coffee tray: "Can you hold the door? My hands are full."
- Fake badge: flash quickly while walking through.
- Delivery: "Delivery for Mr. Smith — which floor?"
- Contractor: "I'm here to fix the HVAC system."
- Smoker re-entry: join smoking group returning inside.
```

### Props

Fake badge / lanyard, appropriate attire, clipboard, work-order paperwork, branded jacket/shirt, tools, confidence and purpose.

## Verifying success

- Reach a documented zone (lobby → secured floor → server room).
- Photograph zone-marker in place, with timestamp.
- Capture observed access controls (mantrap presence, guard challenge, badge reader interaction).

## Common pitfalls

- Engaging in confrontation with a challenger — disengage and document.
- Getting trapped without a phone-out plan — pre-arrange safe-word and exit number.
- Forgetting the rules of engagement — never bring real exploit gear, never disrupt operations.

## Tools

- Badge mock-up + lanyard
- Tester's authorization letter (for emergency stop)
- Camera / phone for evidence
- Pre-agreed safe-word with on-site contact
